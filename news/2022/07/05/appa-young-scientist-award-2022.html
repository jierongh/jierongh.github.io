2022 Asia Pacific Protein Association Young Scientist Award

  • July 05, 2022

The 2022 APPA Young Scientist Award winner is Dr. Rui Zhou of Tsinghua University, Beijing, China. Dr. Rui Zhou was chosen from a group of outstanding scientists from around the Asia Pacific region who had many high impact publications between them.

Dr. Zhou’s research has focused on the structural basis of γ-secretase processing of proteins, particularly Aβ to release β-amyloid peptides, which accumulates in plaques in Alzheimer’s Disease. This work involved solving cryo-electron microscopic structures of γ-secretase, including those in complex with β-amyloid peptides and Notch targets, to see what unique features may exist in β-amyloid peptide binding that would allow development of specific drugs for application to Alzheimer’s Disease. She has published first author papers in the high impact journals Nature, Science and Cell, along with the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, Current Opinion in Structural Biology and Methods in Enzymology. She presented this outstanding work in the APPA Young Scientist Award lecture at the Asia Biophysics Association—Asia Pacific Protein Association—Taiwan Biophysics Society Joint Congress between June 22–25, 2022.

Congratulations to Dr. Rui Zhou and all the other outstanding applicants who have produced ground-breaking work as well.
